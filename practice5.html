About the FIVB

The Fédération Internationale de Volleyball (FIVB) is the international governing body for the sport of volleyball, responsible for overseeing all aspects of the game worldwide. Established in 1947 and headquartered in Lausanne, Switzerland, the FIVB organizes major international competitions such as the Volleyball World Championships, World Cup, and World League. The organization also sets the rules and regulations for the sport, promotes its development globally, and works to enhance its popularity through various initiatives and partnerships.

History of Volleyball

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan, a physical education instructor in Holyoke, Massachusetts, USA. Originally called "Mintonette," the game was designed as a less strenuous alternative to basketball for older members of the YMCA. The first official game took place on July 7, 1896. The sport quickly gained popularity, leading to the establishment of the Fédération Internationale de Volleyball (FIVB) in 1947. Volleyball has since evolved into a globally recognized sport, featuring both indoor and beach variations, and is now an Olympic discipline since 1964.
